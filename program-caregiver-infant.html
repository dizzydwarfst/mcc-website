Caregiver Infant Diploma

Train to support the earliest, most formative years of a child's life. Learn the developmental, safety, and communication skills BC daycares and family-care employers expect.

32 weeks Includes practicum PTIB accredited
About the Program

What is the Caregiver Infant Diploma?

This diploma equips students to support infants and toddlers in licensed daycares, family child-care homes, and private caregiver arrangements. The program covers early-childhood theory, developmental milestones, safety and nutrition, and the communication skills needed to work alongside families and licensed educators.

A supervised practicum at a partner Vancouver childcare centre gives students real classroom hours and a credible reference for employment.

Why It Matters

Why infant care, why now?

BC has a childcare crisis

Provincial waitlists for infant spaces are years long. Trained infant caregivers are among the most-requested workers in the BC labour market.

Work that lasts a lifetime

The first 1,000 days shape a child's emotional and cognitive foundation. Skilled caregivers are part of that foundation.

Pathway to licensure

The diploma stacks toward the BC Early Childhood Educator (ECE) credential and additional Infant/Toddler certification.

Curriculum

What you'll study

Module 1 · Foundations of Early Care

  • Theories of early childhood development
  • Attachment, bonding, and trust
  • Professional ethics and boundaries
  • Child rights and BC licensing standards

Module 2 · Infant Development

  • 0–36 month developmental milestones
  • Brain development and stimulation
  • Language emergence
  • Recognizing developmental delays

Module 3 · Health, Safety & Nutrition

  • Infant feeding and formula handling
  • Safe sleep and SIDS prevention
  • Pediatric first aid and CPR
  • Sanitation and infection control

Module 4 · Play-Based Learning

  • Sensory and motor development
  • Designing safe, age-appropriate play
  • Observing and documenting growth
  • Outdoor and emergent curriculum

Module 5 · Working with Families

  • Family-centred caregiving
  • Cultural awareness in BC
  • Daily reporting and communication
  • Difficult conversations and concerns

Practicum · 200 Supervised Hours

  • Placement at licensed BC daycare
  • Supervised by certified ECE
  • Real classroom participation
  • Employment-ready evaluation
