Global Management in Hospitality Diploma

Join a growing industry and gain opportunities worldwide through a management-focused diploma built for hospitality operations, leadership, and career mobility.

12–16 months 15 courses · 45 credits PTIB accredited
About the Program

What is the Global Management in Hospitality Diploma?

This two-year diploma examines the various management roles in the hospitality industry and develops the skills to match those roles. Students gain industry-specific competence in computer applications, financial management, room division management, food and beverage management, and marketing and sales.

The program combines applied hospitality management training with a clear pathway into a degree program through MCC's university partner, helping students build practical leadership skills for hospitality markets in Canada and beyond.

Why Choose This Program

Management training with a clear pathway

PTIB-accredited program

Students complete a structured diploma program through Metropolitan Community College, with outcomes aligned to hospitality management practice.

MCC diploma credential

Graduates receive a Metropolitan Community College diploma that reflects applied study in operations, finance, marketing, and hospitality leadership.

Degree pathway

With the right pathway, graduates can continue toward a degree through MCC's university partner and keep building their business and management foundation.

Curriculum Areas

What you'll study

Room Division Management

  • Front office operations
  • Housekeeping standards
  • Reservations and revenue

Food & Beverage Management

  • Restaurant and banquet ops
  • Cost control and pricing
  • Service standards

Financial Management

  • Budgeting and forecasting
  • Performance metrics
  • Reporting and controls

Marketing & Sales

  • Hospitality marketing fundamentals
  • Distribution and channels
  • Group and corporate sales

Computer Applications

  • Property management systems
  • POS and back-office tools
  • Data and analytics basics

Leadership & People

  • Team-based teaching model
  • Supervisory skills
  • Service culture and ethics

Program details

  • 96 weeks full-time / part-time
  • 15 courses · 45 credits · 960 contact hours
  • In-class delivery at our Vancouver campus
  • Intakes: Fall, Winter, and Summer

Admission

  • Completion of high school OR 18+ as a mature student
  • IELTS 5.5 or equivalent (e.g. TOEFL IBT)
  • OR completion of MCC's Business Communication / EPAS preparatory programs
  • OR pass the College's language proficiency exam at 70%

Tuition & fees

  • Application fee: CAD 200
  • Domestic tuition: CAD 19,350 + CAD 1,800 materials
  • International tuition: CAD 25,650 + CAD 1,800 materials
  • Eligible for StudentAid BC (SABC)
Important to Know

Application & graduation

Application deadline

Apply at least 4 weeks prior to the start of classes. Late admission may be possible based on intake — speak with admissions early.

Graduation requirement

Successfully complete all required courses. Note: this program is not eligible for a Post-Graduation Work Permit.
