About / Policies

Institutional policies that protect students and uphold standards

MCC publishes the policies that govern student rights, conduct, fees, and dispute resolution. Each policy is available on request through the Registrar's office.

10 published policies PTIB compliant Available on request
Policy Library

All MCC policies in one place

Each policy below is published in full as a PDF document. Contact the Registrar's office at info@metropolitancollege.ca to request a copy.

Respectful and Fair Treatment of Students

MCC's non-discrimination policy. Defines fair, respectful treatment of every student regardless of background.

Safety Policy

Campus safety expectations, including emergency procedures and shared responsibility for a safe learning environment.

Sexual Misconduct Policy

How MCC prevents, responds to, and supports students through reports of sexual misconduct on campus or in college activities.

Student Attendance Policy

Attendance expectations and what counts as authorized absence. Mandatory from the first to final day of each semester.

Student Dismissal Policy

The grounds and process by which a student may be dismissed from MCC, and the rights protected during the process.

Dispute Resolution Policy

How students raise concerns or formal complaints, and the steps MCC takes to resolve them fairly and promptly.

Student Statement of Rights

The rights every MCC student is entitled to under PTIB regulations and MCC's own institutional commitments.

Privacy Policy

How MCC collects, uses, and stores personal information — and how students can access or correct their records.

Refund Policy

Refund eligibility, timing, and process for tuition and other fees, in line with PTIB requirements.

Critical Incident and Crisis Management Policy

How MCC prepares for, responds to, and recovers from critical incidents that affect students, staff, or operations.

Need a copy?

Request any policy document directly from the Registrar

Each policy is provided as a PDF. We are happy to send the document, walk you through it, or schedule a meeting if you have questions about how a policy applies to your situation.
