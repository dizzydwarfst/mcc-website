March 2, 2026
TCF Canada Preparation Program Officially Launches
MCC's new TCF Canada Preparation Program launches this month, completing our French certification suite alongside TEF Canada Prep.
Read More

Hello with crisp winter greetings from MCC. This March marks another milestone in our French-language journey: the official launch of the TCF Canada Preparation Program. Joining our well-established TEF Canada Prep, the new TCF program now gives our students complete coverage of the two main federally recognized French assessments for immigration and academic admission.

The program is built around the four TCF components - reading, listening, writing, and speaking - with simulation-heavy workshops and feedback from instructors with examiner experience. Students may now choose the assessment that best matches their goals, or prepare for both.

This launch closes a multi-year arc that began with our 2024 Alliance Francaise partnership. From Niveau 1 through C1-level mastery, the pathway is now genuinely complete.

October 6, 2025
TEF Canada Preparation Program Officially Launches
MCC introduces its TEF Canada Preparation Program, designed for immigration, university admission, and federal employment goals.
Read More

Hello, with great pride, from the entire MCC team. This October, we proudly launched our newest academic offering: the TEF Canada Preparation Program. This marks the culmination of months of careful curriculum design and establishes MCC as a premier destination for formal French assessment preparation on the West Coast.

Tailored for students targeting immigration to Francophone regions, federal employment, or admission to Francophone universities, the program covers all four TEF components: reading, listening, writing, and speaking. Many of our instructors have direct experience as official examiners.

Both 8-week intensive and 12-week standard formats are available, working in synergy with our ESL French Niveaux 1 through 4. Students are not only learning French - they are now expertly equipped to translate that bilingualism into recognized credentials.
