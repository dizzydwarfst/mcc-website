Caregiver Senior Diploma

Train for one of British Columbia's most in-demand professions: providing dignified, skilled, person-centred care for older adults in homes, assisted living, and long-term care settings.

32 weeks Includes practicum PTIB accredited
About the Program

What is the Caregiver Senior Diploma?

This diploma prepares students to work confidently in residential, community, and assisted-living environments supporting older adults. The program blends classroom instruction in anatomy, gerontology, and person-centred care with hands-on lab simulations and a supervised practicum at a partner BC care facility.

Graduates leave with the technical skills, communication maturity, and cultural awareness expected by Canadian employers in the elder care sector.

Why It Matters

Why senior care, why now?

Canada is aging fast

By 2030, nearly one in four Canadians will be over 65. The senior care workforce gap in BC alone is in the tens of thousands of roles.

Stable, meaningful work

Caregiving is a recession-resilient field. Demand stays steady regardless of the broader economy, and the work is deeply human.

A clear immigration pathway

Senior care is recognized in several federal and BC provincial nominee programs — an important consideration for international students.

Earning Potential

What graduates earn in Canada

Salary ranges reflect typical Canadian market rates as of 2026.

Entry Level

$42K – $52K

First role within 6 months of graduation. Care aide, home support, assisted living attendant.

Experienced

$54K – $68K

Two to four years in. Senior care aide, lead support worker, shift lead.

Specialist & Advanced

$70K – $95K+

With LPN or RN credentials. Practical nurse, care manager, dementia specialist.

Curriculum

What you'll study

Module 1 · Foundations of Care

  • Person-centred care principles
  • Professional ethics and boundaries
  • Canadian healthcare system basics
  • Privacy and PHIPA/PIPA standards

Module 2 · Anatomy & Aging

  • Body systems and aging changes
  • Common chronic conditions
  • Medication awareness (non-administering)
  • Nutrition and hydration for seniors

Module 3 · Practical Skills Lab

  • Safe transfers and mobility aids
  • Personal care and hygiene support
  • Infection prevention and PPE
  • Emergency response and first aid

Module 4 · Mental Health & Dementia

  • Recognizing dementia and delirium
  • De-escalation and gentle redirection
  • Supporting families and caregivers
  • Mental health awareness in seniors

Module 5 · Communication & Culture

  • Cross-cultural caregiving in BC
  • Documentation and reporting
  • Working in interdisciplinary teams
  • Difficult conversations and grief

Practicum · 200 Supervised Hours

  • Placement at partner BC facility
  • Supervised by registered staff
  • Real care plan participation
  • Employment-ready evaluation
